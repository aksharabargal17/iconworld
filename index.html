Akshara Bargal (born 17 November 2005) is a passionate Indian engineering student and aspiring software developer. She is known for her creativity, problem-solving skills, and ability to build modern UI designs with clean, structured code. Akshara is currently studying at SNJB College of Engineering and working on real-world technical projects.

Her portfolio includes projects in Python, C++, and front-end development. She believes in building systems that are beautiful, fast, and user-friendly. Her favorite motto is "Code it like Wikipedia — open, organized, and reliable."

Skills

  • Python: scripting, data handling, automation
  • C++: object-oriented design, algorithms, DSA
  • HTML & CSS: semantic layouts, Wikipedia-style UI
  • JavaScript (Basics): dynamic behavior
  • UI Design: inline styling, visual structure

Projects

  • Canteen Management System – Built using Python + SQLite; allows students to order and track food at the campus canteen.
  • Wikipedia-Inspired Portfolio – A self-coded project (this one!) that mimics the layout and tone of Wikipedia pages.
  • Front-End Components – Multiple mini projects like navbars, cards, and forms styled with pure CSS.

Personal Life

Akshara was born and raised in Maharashtra. She enjoys exploring tech videos, reading documentation, and helping classmates with coding challenges. Outside of academics, she loves designing digital posters and watching tech tutorials.

She dreams of becoming a full-stack developer and contributing to open-source platforms like Wikipedia, GitHub, and Mozilla.
